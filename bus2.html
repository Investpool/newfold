Antiretroviral Drugs Stacked at the Tema Port

9th April, 2024

Commodities including antiretroviral drugs to Ghana got stacked due to unresolved issues at the Tema Port. The Global Fund reveals that consignment valued at $40million comprising anti retroviral drugs, treated nets and others have been held up at the Tema port since May 2023 due to government’s insistence on $3.6 millon import duty.


The lead convener Ernest Amoabeng Oteng said the coalition plans to petition the parliament of Ghana and the Presidency on April 17th to address their concern.


According to him, a twelve member delegation from the Global fund paid a working visit to the country to follow up on the…….commodities. In spite of all their efforts, they did not make a headway and the commodities still remained uncleared at the port. We have therefore indicated that Global Fund risks losing all Global Fund support if government fails to act on the matter.


To start with, they have already suspended all commodities shipment to Ghana until the one at the port are cleared.


Adding his voice to this development, the Executive Director of African Health Policy Research and Analysis, Joseph Anaba described government’s posture as sluggish and pointed to lack of prioritization of the health sector.
