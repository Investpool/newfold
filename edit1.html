A FIGHT FOR GHANA’ S NATURAL ENVIRONMENT AND THE GALAMSEY MENACE

At the brinks of time, Galamsey or small scale mining is ending pretty soon. Thanks to the organized labour and the good citizens of Ghana in the pursuit of this daunting task. Governments have failed, and all regulatory authorities have failed.


As hope is on sight, it is imperative to ponder over alternative livelihood measures for the small scale miners, in that, families are not exposed to dangers of hardships.


There is a Yoruba proverb that says – “Responsibility does not devolve on the father only for him to say it is his son’s duty”.


Government has all it takes to know, through digitalization processes, that certain categories of citizens are in parts of the country that may become potential small scale miners. That is to say less effort was made by the state to seek the welfare of millions of citizens in the wild.


Now that farmlands had been destroyed, farms ravaged and water bodies extremely polluted, what’s going to be the consequences of all these?


Social intervention formulae needs to be employed as a matter of urgency to switch the labour required for these miners so that the effect of redundancy is not activated to breed more problems. Constitutionally, they have rights to work and to live.


The social intervention can take the forms of international and government grants for speedy resettlement purposes.


We must remember every able bodied citizen of a sound mind is an asset to the state.
