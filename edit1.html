THE PRESIDENT'S DECISION ON THE INDEPENDENCE DAY CELEBRATION

It has been a worthy decision by the President to call off the Independence Day Celebration.


Though it’s a cost cutting mechanism on the part of the State, it must not necessarily be the case at all times. A case in point is all services and products that might have been used for economic enhancement to the private sector may not be used, rendering products markets inactive within the period.


In any case, it’s good, looking at the current circumstances of reconstructing of the economy.


This must not be the only measure adopted by the President that becomes dear to the hearts of Ghanaians. Another monstrous cost cutting potential out there to be considered is the gargantuan wage packages paid to political office holders – Parliamentarians, Ministers, Appointees and the like.


If critical consideration is giving to that, all citizens would feel being treated equally and have no choice but die for the country in whichever endeavor they find themselves.


Frankly, such crazy animal farm syndrome associated with Ghana’s political class kills the spirits of many. The end result is the lack of patriotism in different forms that we all experience today. A stitch in time saves nine.


Akosua Yirenkyi

13th February, 2025
