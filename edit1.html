HOT HEADEDNESS INTO PUBLIC PLACES

Hot headedness is an ingredient that abounds in our communities in particular and in our country in general.


Some positive values are sometimes displayed by people associated with hotheadedness but most times they are an albatros on the neck of families and at large, a problem to the entire society.


The happenings in parliament of Ghana is a case in point where wisdom is completely overshadowed by hot headedness.


The people who these people represent must be ashamed. They may ask, "did we send him to destroy furniture or damage microphones"?. An outsider may suggest, if this is the best person in the constituency then that area of town exists for trouble.


It is the duty of citizens to test the psyche of people they send to parliament to represent them. By this, utmost peace would be assured in all dealings for real progress in our society.


Linda Anderson

1st January, 2025
