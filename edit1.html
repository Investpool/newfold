STRATEGIC PROPOSALS FROM THE WIDER GHANAIAN PUBLIC ON THE ECONOMY

“Ghana is hard”, a common jargon on the mouth of the majority of Ghanaians takes its root from the fact that food becomes an issue in many homes due to lower agricultural output, unfair distribution of national cake and imbalances in the job market.


For the nation to see a boost in its economic indices, bold steps need to be considered following the plea of the President to the workers of Ghana to sacrifice in the face of demands for higher pay.


A family of five feeding on kenkey for a super spends 50 Ghana cedis. That’s 5 cedis fish each and 5 cedis kenkey each. Assuming all meals cost the same, that would be 150 Ghana cedis a day. For a 30 day cycle, 4,500 Ghana cedis go for food alone.


Simply put, increasing agricultural output by a greater margin equally needs a sacrifice from the political class. When food prices and other agricultural raw materials become cheaper, it has a rippling effect on all sectors of the economy.


This year, car loans and fat allowances paid to all political functionaries must be converted to acquire agricultural machineries (tractors). Getting 1000 new tractors distributed in all agricultural enclaves of the country is the best bet for increasing food production among others.


At the end of the day, we can be proud of those who voted themselves to die for the nation, those who are determined to see Ghana as better place in the community of nations and those who resolve to be selfless in their pursuit to good governance.


By Josephine Essel

23rd March, 2025
