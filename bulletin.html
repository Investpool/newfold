Public reactions over the 1 cedi energy levy slapped by the NDC government has aroused several sentiments and would soon die down. It looks like those in favour are those that are against the management style of the erstwhile NPP government. Their argument is simple. "You have accumulated so much debt and someone finds ways and means to pay them, what is your take?"

Transport operators too agued, "You did not discuss any matter concerning the levy with us, only to do that at our blind side. We would not agree."

Those on the side of wisdom say, the President means well by imploring the citizens to take the move as a bitter pill because if its not done, the consequencies would be dire. One commentator argued on a platform that "in London, one pays higher taxes for the least transaction you can imagine but citizens are happy to pay because such monies create an excellent system for human beings to live in." He cited an instance where even an open space in front of your house is paid for on hourly bases when you park a car.

So i ask a question to conclude the matter. "Is it not because foreign systems are built well with taxes, that our leaders pride themselves in travelling to those places?" If the answer is yes, ladies and gentlemen, let's allow the government of the day to do their work now that good signs are clearly written on the wall - Inflation brought down to 18 percent, exchange rate in its lowest of almost 10 cedis to the dollar, transport fares reduced and prices of few goods reduced.
