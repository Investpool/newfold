GHANA's PUPLIC DEBT REDUCING STURDILY

25th July, 2025

By News Desk

In every space of time, having the ability to pay debts to creditors is nourishing especially in the midst of positively looking back at progress made out of the debt incurred.

Increasing public debt of Ghana has been a daunting development facing governments in the past and now. As some proclaim its the trend and nothing can be done about it, others believed prudent management and discipline can turn situations around once the roads to "killers'" pockets are completely sealed.

In a statement made to parliament on Thursday 24th July, the Finance Minister Dr Cassiel Ato Forson announced that the public debt of Ghana has reduced from Ghc 726.7 billion at December 2024 to Ghc 613 billion by June ending 2025. A drop of Ghc 113 billion within six months of NDC administration.

It is fundamentally believed that With minimal public debt to service, lots of receivables may go into infrastructural development that translates into enhanced standard of living for all.
