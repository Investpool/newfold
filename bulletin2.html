Basic School Pupils Overburdened With Too Many Subjects

15th December, 2025

By News Desk

Pupils in Basic Schools across the country are facing an unprecedented academic workload, with some tackling up to eight subjects, leading to concerns about the physical and mental toll on these young learners.

Reports have emerged of pupils as young as six years old lugging heavy school bags, packed with textbooks and exercise books for subjects including Mathematics, English, Science, Social Studies, History, Computing, Creative Arts, Career Technology and even additional languages like French and Ghanaian languages. The weight of these bags has raised alarms among parents, educators, and health experts, who warn of potential long-term health implications, including back pain, muscle strain, and spinal deformities.

"I can't even lift my child's bag, it's so heavy," lamented a concerned parent. "They're complaining of back pain and fatigue, it's heartbreaking to see them struggle like this."

The Ghana Education Service (GES) has been urged to review the curriculum and reduce the number of subjects, to prioritize quality over quantity and ensure a healthier learning environment.

Children should be allowed to play, explore, and learn without the burden of excessive academic pressure. They must be safeguard for their physical and mental well-being.

Sections of the public are advocating for a maximum of 4 subjects at the basic level, preferably Mathematics, English Language, Science and Ghanaian Language. In their view, subjects such as Social Studies, History, Career Technology and Creative Art can be redistributed to form part of the English course and the Ghanaian language. With these changes teachers on the other hand can be re-deployed to industries as a result of less numbers of subjects needed.

As the nation awaits a response from the GES, parents and educators are left to grapple with the consequences of an overburdened curriculum, and the impact on the nation's future leaders.
