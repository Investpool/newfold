    DSC Technical Institute
    in the midst of Collapse

    Our communities hold a special place in our childhood memories. Family relationships, neighbourhoods and infrastructure are just a few of the things we remember growing up in our various communities.

    Along the way, families relocate to other cities in search of jobs and other attractions. The most common factors underlying these migrations are urbanisation, prestige which comes along with economic and social modernization.

    This leaves the countryside in the hands of the elderly and those who don’t travel because they can’t afford to, and because they don’t have any connections in the cities.

    In perspective, communities must be showcased with all potentials for growth, be it social or economic. It's sad to note that most communties that nurtured our generations in past and now for its splendour and love are now left behind, deserted and uncared for.

    Ghana has a strong social heritage of fellow feeling, a trait that renders our culture enviable by the international community and all races. What is missing is psycho-analytical stature of leadership in all levels, in that no citizen is left behind in our collective pursuit of happiness and dignity.

    The ultimate is to ensure a balanced regional development where all places throughout the country are enhanced, befitting a human status.

    What makes the difference?
