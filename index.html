    • Our communities hold a special place in our childhood memories. Family relationships, neighbourhoods and infrastructure are just a few of the things we remember growing up in our various communities.

    Along the way, families relocate to other cities in search of jobs and other attractions. The most common factors underlying these migrations are urbanisation, prestige which comes along with economic and social modernization.

    This leaves the countryside in the hands of the elderly and those who don’t travel because they can’t afford to, and because they don’t have any connections in the cities.

    In perspective, communities must be showcased with all potentials for growth, be it social or economic. Its sad to note that most communties that nurtured our generations in past and now for its splendour and love are now left behind, deserted and uncared for.

    Looking back, its worth considering a special attention paid to our source of inspiration and growth. Coming back to our places of birth after a long working years would in one way or the other give encouragement to the young ones to do same. The ultimate is to ensure a balanced regional development where all places throughout the country are enhanced, befitting a human status.

    Its the duty of our educated youths to eschew selfishness and speak for the voiceless in the areas of general concern, especially the responsibility of the central government.

    The proverbial Kofi Mensah from Dokrochiwa, who was nurtured by the toils of his kinsmen now pays so much tax to the government. His root of origin is left to His people who till this day share the same creek waters with cattle and other animals. Indeed citizens and the state must contribute to the growth of every community.

    GREATER ACCRA REGION

    Accra is a cosmopolitan area and the capital city of Ghana. There are diverse economic activities in the Region ranging from medium scale manufacturing industries owned by multi nationals, to small scale (one-man) manufacturing hubs. The Shai and Dangme areas too record a little agricultural production.

    The area of interest to NEWSBOX is the small scale entities. These hubs are identified and mapped for a possible recommendations, support and expansion.

    CENTRAL REGION

    By popular opinion, the Central Region is estimated to be one of the poorest regions in the country. This is obvious on account of high rate of teenage pregnancy as recorded by the National statistics data of 2020.

    There are potentials for high agro industrial output as its the hub for pineaple and potato production especially on the Gomoa plains and parts of Awutu District. Factories for converting these crops into secondary products is the game changer as it employs hundreds of teenage mothers for a life time engagement.

    EASTERN REGION

    Eastern Region is a resource rich part of Ghana. From mining activities to small scale agriculture in forest zones and that of Volta plains. Fishing activities in the Afram Plains area cannot be left out.

    One notable area of interest is the fisheries sector, where much investment can be made in cat fish and tilapia production. Processes are underway for a research into those productive ventures especially along the Volta River.

    WESTERN REGION

    Western Region is the hub of all energy production activities including other petrochemical entities. Thanks to an average production capacities of gas that runs our thermal engines for the supply of electricity to households.

    In addition, the forest nature of the environment promises a high level of raw material productions for a good industrial considerations. Eyes are wide opened to see what comes out of our researches and engagements.

    WESTERN NORTH REGION

    This Region is a break away part of Western Region as a result of government's programm aimed at making administrative functions closer to the people. It shares same productive values with the Western Region. Detailed outlook is under way to establish its productive capacity vis a vis the national output.

    BONO REGION

    The region is a satisfying ecological enclave with an optimum primary productive activities. Unfortunately, less industrial establishments are spotted in this area of the country.

    This is believed to be associated with the distance from the administrative capital, Accra coupled with poor networked roads.
    BONO EAST REGION

    The Volta River makes a lot of the environment in which the Bono East is situated. Much more can be done with the fishing industry in this area. Rigorous attention is paid to revealing the true state of the region for sustainable development

    AHAFO REGION

    Ahafo shares the same ecological features with Bono Region. Yam, Tomatoes and maize production is the main agricultural fits of the region. The impact of Government's role in the region would be assessed and reported to stakeholders.

    NORTHERN REGION

    Northern Region is a developing area by means of infrastructural add ups in the capital city, Tamale and its environs. Up north is a vast agricultural lands that has all it takes to qualify the region for grain basket of the entire country, especially in the area of guinea corn production.

    Its believed that when greater percentage of the productive activities are mechanized and the irrigation status of White Volta is totally established, things would change for the better.

    UPPER WEST REGION

    Upper West Region is the northwesterly part of Ghana and a home for many renowned personalities in the productive agricultural space.

    As a hub of fair natural resources, the region has its major industries in animal and food crop productions. National statistics does not indicate the quantum of these production levels vis a vis the national averages. However its worthy of note that sufficient productions are made for local consumptions and export to neighboring countries.

    NEWSBOX in furthering its coverage of the region, projects to make account of the rate and the level at which these raw primary products are made and the percentage that is changed into secondary products. Personalities championing the productive industries would be promoted in such a way that more room is created for them to excel.

    The number of industrial establishments in the regions is equally an important concern for our assessment. Through this, we hope to provide adequate infographics for public consumption and investment processes.

    UPPER EAST REGION

    Upper East Region with Bawku as a buzzling marketing center links two international frontiers.

    Different kinds of products, both industrial and agricultural find their way into the region. This is associated with the fact that it connects the sahel to the south.

    It is therefore apparent that much smoother commercial links to the region, by way of ownership and management of supply chains are needed to ensure a sustainable business environment as well as pricing modalities.

    VOLTA REGION

    Volta Region has the potential of feeding the entire nation with its rice production capabilities. Its noted that about seven varieties of rice are grown in the region with great looks and nutritive values.

    Unfortunately the farmers use agrarian technologies accounting for very low output. Mechanization is projected to be enhanced to a remarkable rate along side complete marketing strategies.

    Details of industrial activities in this area would be made available to stakeholders in due course.
