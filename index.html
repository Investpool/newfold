LET'S REACH OUT TO OUR COMMUNITIES IN GHANA

Our communities hold a special place in our childhood memories. Family relationships, neighbourhoods and infrastructure are just a few of the things we remember growing up in our various communities.

Along the way, families relocate to other cities in search of jobs, other attractions, and even the simple act of relocating. The most common factor underlying these migrations is urbanisation, which entails economic and social modernization.

This leaves the countryside in the hands of the elderly and those who don’t travel because they can’t afford to, because they don’t have any connections in the cities.

In perspective, communities must be showcased with all potentials for growth, be it social or economic.

    UPPER WEST REGION

    Upper West Region is the northwesterly part of Ghana and a home for many renowned personalities in the productive agricultural space.

    As a hub of fair natural resources, the region has its major industries in animal and food crop productions. National statistics does not indicate the quantum of these production levels vis a vis the national averages. However its worthy of note that sufficient productions are made for local consumptions and export to neighboring countries.

    NEWSBOX in furthering its coverage of the region, projects to make account of the rate and the level at which these raw primary products are made and the percentage that is changed into secondary products. Personalities championing the productive industries would be promoted in such a way that more room would be created for them to excel.

    The number of industrial establishments in the regions is equally an important concern for our assessment. Through this, we hope to provide adequate infographics for public consumption and investment processes.

    UPPER EAST REGION

    Upper east region with Bawku as its capital is a buzzling marketing center due to divers links to two international frontiers.

    Different kinds of products, both industrial and agricultural find their way into the region. This is associated with the fact that it connects the sahel to the south.

    It is therefore apparent that much smoother commercial links to the region, by way of ownership and management of supply chains can ensure a sustainable business environment.
