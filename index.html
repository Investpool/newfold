LET'S REACH OUT TO OUR COMMUNITIES IN GHANA

Our communities share an important memories in our up-bringing as children. Family relations, neighbourhoods and infrastructural environments are the few to recall when growing up as children in our various communities.

Along the line, families move to other destinations in pursuit of jobs, other attractions and even the simple ectasy of relocation. The most undertone for these movements are urbanisation which comes with economic and social status enhancement.

In effect, the countryside is left to the mercy of old people and those who do not have the opportunity to travel because they may not have any connection in the cities.

In perspective, communities must be showcased with all potentials for growth, be it social or economic.

What makes the difference?
