FULL EMPLOYMENT IN GHANA'S PERSPECTIVE

'There is no work in Ghana'. We say there's work in Ghana depending on the lens through which we see the naratives from various angles.

The early economists as Keynes postulates full employment as a yard-stick for absolute quality of life. It further asserts that any able bodied person who wants to work can find employment.


Work, in simple terms is the combination of all factors of production that brings about an output, which are goods and services consumed by the masses. In our part of the world in general and Ghana in particular, so much emphasis is paid to labour within the chain of these factors that all the others are barely considered.


The following points show details of all factors worth considering:
Labour - the human resources (workmen, engineers, lawyers, teachers, medics, administrators)
Land - rivers, lakes, seas forests agricultural lands, mineral deposits, air space.
Capital - man-made resources acquired by use of money.
Entrepreneurship - organisational resources for management and coordination (governments and individuals).


Our problem for not attaining full employment is the fact that less projection is made for the quality of life for the average Ghanaian.



If Koohia a farmer in the Atiwa District is deemed fit to at least, live in a decent self owned house, it should take the government as the chief entrepreneur to coordinate easily all the rest of the factors of production to his advantage that's making him produce better. In doing these, the subsequent action is that other factors are going to be activated as they are needed to complete the cycle.

The function of the government in this case is to coordinate for the betterment of all citizens in a frank manner. Nothing else.


A positive outlook at the above discussion renders all people happy and productive for a Great Nation.
