EDITORIAL

RIVER GUARD RECRUITMENTS IS NO WHERE NEAR THE END TO GALAMSEY

Be it a state, organizations or individuals, four fundamental principles that plunder us into absolute development are action, inaction, commission and omission of decisions. Beyond these, there is nothing than knowledge as the bedrock on which the four thrives.


Knowledge abounds in Ghana, looking at what our universities and other educational institutions churn out on yearly bases. Nonetheless, our professors and other professionals are remarkable. What matters is the index in which the four parameters described above are applied under particular circumstance.


We make a case for the recruitment of River Guards in the pursuit of ending galamsey in the country. This is indeed an action. But the question is can it help or not. If not, inaction, premised on this decision is the most appropriate index to adopt. Certainly, it is a very bad action taken by the government. First, joint military and police did same and were largely not successful. What can we say about a mere unarmed River Guard?


For popular opinion, these young men and women must rather be considered for an enterprise around agriculture, where short term training is given to them to operate machineries and equipment. As it becomes sustainable and food production volumes increase, all things would fall in place. That’s where thousands of people engaged in Galamsey would relinquish their “old job” and join the masses in the new agricultural reality.


In this case, inaction would have won the bid for that decision.


By Richard Eshun

28rd March, 2025
